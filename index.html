Baghdad

Brief Overview and History

At the top of the page, we have different links for things we think you should, do, places you should go and more.

Baghdad was established in 8th century and was an economic center until its fall in about 1200 AD. Baghdad was established because the Abbasids wanted a new capital, and to increase their dawla, or amount of land. Al-Saffāḥ tried the cities of Hās̲h̲imiyya and Al-Manṣūr, however both were vulnerable to attackers and were too distracting to his army. He was looking for a new city, and wanted it to succeed Babylon and all the other big cities at that time. He chose the site of Baghdad because it had a fertile plain, and was near a river and on the Kudasan road. It had a good temperature and not a major mosquito problem.
