Religion

The three main religions in Baghdad were Islam, Judaism and Christianity, who lived in the northern part of Baghdad, north of of Sūḳ al-G̲h̲azl, and west of of Sūḳ al-G̲h̲azl respectively. Among the three, Arabic was the common language spoken. Most of the Persians lived on the west side. Of these religions, Islam seemed to be a religion of the elite more than the other 2 (cerca ~900's CE). [1] The Mosque, or main center of worship was also a very important building during this time. In addition to being the main center of worship, it was also the head of religious and political life.
